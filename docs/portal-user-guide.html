1. About the portal

The UssdGatewayTz operator portal is the web UI on top of the USSD gateway. It lets you watch traffic in flight, drill into individual sessions, audit operator behaviour, manage shortcode routing, and grant other people access. The data shown here is the same data the gateway logs to its database — there is nothing live-faked.

What you can do with the portal depends on your role (see section 2). Most users land on Overview, drill into Sessions or Session Hops, and (occasionally) queue an export. Super Admins additionally manage shortcodes, operators, and other portal users.

Tip — the portal never modifies USSD traffic. Everything on the read side is a query against an audit table; everything on the write side (shortcodes, operators, users) reconfigures routing, not customer sessions.

2. Roles & permissions

The portal recognises four roles. Each has a different sidebar; you see only the routes your role can reach.

RoleBest forCan readCan change
Super Admin Operations lead, gateway maintainer Every page, every shortcode, every session, every audit row Shortcodes, operators, all portal users, exports
Auditor Compliance, security review Same read scope as Super Admin (all shortcodes, all users) plus the audit log Nothing — write actions are blocked even though the list pages render
Admin (Client) Shortcode owner; the partner running a service code Reports, sessions, and exports for their own shortcodes only One viewer per Admin account (read-only delegate on their shortcodes)
Viewer Helpdesk / second-pair-of-eyes on an Admin's shortcodes Reports, sessions, and exports for the shortcodes their Admin granted them Nothing

Under the hood: roles are seeded in db/001_init.sql and db/010_roles_auditor_viewer.sql. Permission keys are listed in dashboard/src/lib/rbac.ts.

Why two read-only roles? Auditor reads globally; Viewer reads only the shortcodes its Admin explicitly granted. An Auditor sees every customer's session; a Viewer sees only what their client allowed.

3.1 URL

The portal is served from the gateway's host on Kong's HTTPS port (the same host that handles USSD inbound). Your Super Admin will share the exact URL — typically:

3.2 Sign in

Enter your email and password. After six failed attempts in a row you are locked out for 30 minutes — wait it out or ask a Super Admin to clear the lock.

3.3 Forgot password

The login screen has a Forgot password? link. The portal sends a one-time reset link to your registered email; the link expires in 30 minutes. If your email is wrong, only a Super Admin can change it.

3.4 First-time login

If you were just created, the Super Admin will have either (a) emailed you a setup link, or (b) handed you a temporary password. In case (b), change it immediately from the user-detail page (Portal users → me).

3.5 Signing out

Top-right of the topbar. Sign out on shared machines — sessions last 8 hours by default.

4. Around the screen

4.1 Sidebar

The dark left rail lists every page your role can reach. The active page is highlighted in red. The sidebar is collapsible on mobile (top-right menu icon).

4.2 Top bar

Shows your name, role, and the sign-out button on the right. On mobile, navigation collapses into the top bar.

4.3 Filter bar

Reports, Sessions, Session Hops and Summary share a filter strip:

  • MNO — Vodacom, Airtel, Tigo, Halotel (the operators known to the gateway).
  • Range — Today, Last 24h, Last 7d, This month, or a custom date range.
  • Shortcode — restrict to one of the codes you own / can see.
  • Search (where applicable) — substring match on MSISDN, session ID, or USSD trail.

Filters are sticky: changing the range on Sessions carries through if you click into Session Hops.

4.4 Light / dark theme

Theme toggle is in the top bar. The choice is saved in your browser (no server round-trip).

5. Overview

The default landing page. Headline numbers (today's session count, success rate, average duration) for the shortcodes you have access to, plus a small bar chart of the last few days. Use it as a glance; click any tile or "View all" link to jump into the underlying list.

Visible to: every role.

6. Reports Summary

Pre-aggregated daily roll-up. Each row is one operator × one shortcode × one day, with columns for total sessions, completed sessions, billable sessions, average duration, and error counts. Backed by the materialised view daily_session_summary (refreshed nightly) plus the in-flight today_session_summary_mv.

  • Cheapest page to look at; loads even on a year of data.
  • Use this for monthly billing reconciliation.
  • Click a row to open Sessions filtered to that operator + shortcode + day.

7. Sessions

One row per USSD session (one customer dial). Columns include session ID, MNO, shortcode, MSISDN, started/ended, duration, leg count, outcome (END/timeout/cancelled), the USSD trail (menu path the customer took) and a billable-sessions counter for the MNO's billing window.

  • Expand any row to see all hops in chronological order without leaving the page.
  • Use the search to find a specific customer by MSISDN or a complaint case by session ID.
  • "Billable Sessions" applies the MNO's billing-window rule (e.g. Vodacom's 20-second rolling window).

8. Session Hops

One row per HTTP leg the gateway processed — the most granular view. Useful for diagnosing a specific failure (what the handler returned, what the gateway sent back to the MNO, how long each leg took, whether it timed out).

Typical workflow: a customer complains → look up their session on Sessions → expand it OR jump to Session Hops with the session ID prefilled → identify the failing leg.

9. Download Exports

For data volumes too large to scroll. Queue a CSV export filtered by date range, MNO, shortcode, and outcome; the export runs in the background and a row appears with status queued → running → ready. Click the link to download. Exports expire after seven days.

  • Each export carries the filter set that produced it — re-runnable from the same row.
  • Export size and row count are shown so you know the file is plausible before downloading.
  • You only see your own exports. Super Admin sees everyone's.

10. Integration Document

Reference for partners who own the handler URL configured against a shortcode. Documents the unified JSON contract the gateway POSTs (msisdn, session_id, service_code, ussd_string, event, raw_payload) and what to return (CON … for "continue, prompt for input", END … for "terminate"). MNO-specific quirks (Halotel async, Tigo aggregator shapes) are called out per-section.

11. Admin pages

11.1 Shortcodes Super Admin Auditor (read-only)

The routing table — one row per (operator, code). Columns: operator, code, label, owner (a portal user), handler URL, auth mode, status (active / maintenance / deactivated), and timestamps. Click a row to edit; click "New shortcode" to add one. The Status column controls customer behaviour:

StatusWhat the customer sees
activeNormal flow — the gateway calls the handler URL.
maintenanceAn END message ("Service under maintenance"), no handler call.
deactivatedAn END message ("Service unavailable"), no handler call.

11.2 Operators Super Admin Auditor (read-only)

Catalogue of the MNOs the gateway recognises (vodacom, airtel, tigo, halotel by default). Toggle active to take a whole MNO offline (useful in incident-response). The display name is what the dashboard shows; the canonical name is what the gateway routes by.

11.3 My shortcodes Admin (Client)

The Admin's home for the shortcodes they own. Shows traffic stats per shortcode and links to the related Sessions / Session Hops filtered to that code. Admins manage their viewer from here — see section 12.2.

11.4 Portal users

Behaviour depends on role:

  • Super Admin — full CRUD over every portal user; assign role; activate/deactivate.
  • Auditor — read-only list of all users.
  • Admin (Client) — manages a single Viewer scoped to their own shortcodes (max one per Admin).
You cannot deactivate the last active Super Admin. The portal blocks the action to avoid locking everyone out.

11.5 Audit log Super Admin Auditor

Append-only record of every privileged action: logins, password resets, shortcode edits, user changes, role swaps, exports queued, etc. Each row carries timestamp, actor, action, target, outcome (success/failure/denied), source IP, and a JSONB detail blob with the before/after where relevant.

12. Common tasks

12.1 Find why a customer's session failed

  1. Ask the customer for their phone number or the time the call was made.
  2. Go to Sessions, set the date filter to that day, search by MSISDN.
  3. Click the matching row to expand the hops — look for the leg with a non-success status or non-zero error_class.
  4. If you need raw payloads, click into Session Hops from the expanded row.

12.2 Add a Viewer to your own shortcode (Admin)

  1. Open Portal users.
  2. Click Add viewer — fill in name + email; the portal sends them a setup email.
  3. On the viewer's row, select which of your shortcodes they may see.
  4. Save. The viewer's access is live on their next login.

You can edit the granted-shortcodes list at any time, deactivate the viewer, or delete the row to revoke access entirely.

12.3 Queue a CSV export

  1. From Sessions or Session Hops, set the filter you want.
  2. Click Export in the toolbar — the page redirects to Download Exports with a row in status queued.
  3. Wait — the row flips to ready when finished (typical: under a minute for < 100 000 rows).
  4. Click the file name to download. The file expires after seven days.

12.4 Take a shortcode into maintenance

  1. Open Shortcodes, click the row.
  2. Change Status to maintenance; optionally edit the maintenance message customers will see.
  3. Save. Effect is immediate — the next inbound USSD on that code returns the maintenance END.
  4. Reverse by setting Status back to active.

12.5 Disable a user temporarily

  1. Portal users → the user's row → toggle Active off.
  2. The user's existing session is invalidated within seconds; they cannot sign in again until reactivated.

12.6 Change your own password

  1. Click your name in the top bar → My profile (or open Portal users → click your own row).
  2. Use the Change password action. Minimum length 12 characters; the form will tell you if it's weak.

13. Troubleshooting

SymptomWhat to check
Login fails with "invalid credentials" Caps lock, browser auto-fill stale; after 6 misses you are locked out 30 min. Forgot password as a last resort.
"You don't have permission" The route requires a role/permission your account lacks. Ask a Super Admin to confirm your role.
A shortcode is missing from the dropdown Only shortcodes you own (Admin) or have been granted (Viewer) appear. Super Admin / Auditor see all.
Export stuck in "queued" The export worker may be down — flag a Super Admin. Re-queueing won't help if the worker is the issue.
Counts look smaller than expected Sessions over a week old may have been moved to the archive partition — filter range covers the date window of interest.
"Method not allowed" returned by /ussd/ when you paste it in a browser That endpoint is for MNO POSTs, not interactive use. It's expected.

14. Glossary

AggregatorThe MNO-side appliance that forwards USSD traffic into the gateway over HTTPS.
Billable sessionSessions counted under an MNO's billing-window rule (e.g. Vodacom's 20-second rolling window) — used for invoicing.
CON / ENDThe two action verbs in a handler reply. CON means "more input expected — keep the session open". END terminates the session.
Handler URLThe HTTP endpoint the gateway POSTs to for a given shortcode. Owned by the partner running the service.
Hop / LegOne HTTP round-trip in a session. A typical session has 2–6 hops.
MNOMobile Network Operator. The gateway speaks Vodacom, Airtel, Tigo and Halotel.
MSISDNThe customer's phone number in E.164 format (e.g. 255712345678).
Service codeThe USSD code the customer dialled, e.g. *147*03#.
SessionOne customer dial, from first leg until the handler ends or the MNO times out.
ShortcodeThe gateway's routing-table row that ties (operator, service code) to a handler URL.
USSD trailThe path of menu choices a customer made, written as 1*2*3 meaning "selected 1, then 2, then 3".
